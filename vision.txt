 Based on my investigation of your codebase, I completely understand why the current setup feels tangled. Right now, your system handles Platform Management   
  (SuperAdmin) and Team Management (Tenant Admin) within the exact same database models, API endpoints, and frontend components.

  Here is my proposed architecture and step-by-step plan to reorganize VisualFX Studio into a clean, industry-standard Multi-Tenant Platform.

  ---

  The Proposed Architecture: "Strict Isolation"

  We need to create a strict boundary between Platform Administration (You) and Tenant Administration (Subscribers).

  1. Database Enhancements (Prisma)
  Your current Organization model tracks API keys and pricing, but lacks the specific limits you want to enforce.
   * Add Tenant Limits: Add maxUsers and maxProjectsTotal to the Organization model.
   * Add Identifier: Add an isDefault boolean to cleanly identify your SuperAdmin organization without relying on string matching (name.includes("default")).   

  2. Backend API Separation
  We will split the monolithic /api/admin/* routes into two distinct, isolated routers:
   * SuperAdmin Router (/api/superadmin/*):
       * POST /organizations/tenant: A streamlined endpoint that creates the Organization, sets the limits (maxUsers, maxProjects), and creates the Tenant Admin         user in one single transaction.
       * POST /users/demo: A dedicated endpoint strictly for creating Demo Users. It will hardcode the view to PICDRIFT, assign exactly 5 Picdrift and 15 PicFX 
         credits, and lock them to your Default Org.
       * PUT /organizations/:id/status: To pause/deactivate tenants who haven't paid.
   * Tenant Router (/api/tenant/*):
       * GET /users & POST /users: Strict isolation. When a Tenant Admin hits this, the backend automatically filters by their organizationId and enforces the  
         maxUsers limit. No risk of cross-tenant data leakage.

  3. Frontend UI Split (The Key to Fluidity)
  We will deprecate the confusing AdminDashboard.tsx and split it into two distinct, purpose-built dashboards:

  A. SuperAdminDashboard.tsx (Your Platform Control Center)
   * Tab: Tenants: View all subscribed organizations. A simple "New Tenant" wizard that asks for Org Name -> Limits -> Admin Email/Password in one clean flow.  
     Toggle active/inactive status here.
   * Tab: Demo Leads: A dedicated interface just for managing your Picdrift demo users. One click to generate a demo account with pre-filled credits.
   * Tab: Global Economics: Your global pricing templates.

  B. TenantDashboard.tsx (Subscriber Control Panel)
   * No Picdrift Mention: Subscribers will only see VisualFX terminology.
   * Tab: My Team: They can invite users up to their limit. The dropdown for "Role" will only show USER or MANAGER (they cannot create other Admins).
   * Tab: Integrations: Where they paste their own FAL/OpenAI keys.
